Citation: Fletcher, D., Melin, L., & Gimeno, A. (2012). Culture and Values in Family Business—A Review and Suggestions for Future Research. Journal of Family Business Strategy, 3(3), 127–131.

(p. 128)

As can be seen in this short review, studies in family business culture focus on three aspects:

  • First studies of culture specify ‘what culture is’ or how it can be defined/conceptualised in the family firm context.
  • Second, studies focus on ‘what culture can do’ for the family firm—often with normative/regulative overtones.
  • Third, inquiries identify the ‘factors/attributes that shape culture’ at the same time as examining the effects that cultural processes engender.
